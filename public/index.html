About Me

I have always been a creative person with a mind that is always hungry for more knowledge. Over the years I have focused my attention on many different subjects and skill sets including learning how to play five different musical instruments, fund raising and marketing my own non-profit cultural organization, learning Portuguese, playing music gigs, living on my goat farm in Brazil teaching English and, of course, learning computer coding. I got my bachelors from The New School in Jazz performance, and after having taught music lessons and playing in bands for many years, I started to realize that this source of income was too irregular. I decided to go back to school to get my masters degree in Performing Arts Management and Production.

Just as I was graduated and ready to start my dream of running my own cultural center, the real estate market took a dive along with all resources that would have been the backbone of my program and project funding. Frustrated and disillusioned, I decided to give the corporate world a try while the economy recuperated. I found myself very incredibly bored. There was no variety or learning on the job and the monotony really got to me. I realized how much the corporate structure and bureaucracy made me feel insignificant and I knew I had more to offer than this. I wanted more but wasn't sure exactly what.

My husband and I purchased a 22 acre farm in his home country of Brazil and we decided to make a go of farm life. I was ready for something completely different and the idea of possibly having a chance to play some Brazilian music sounded great. Over the next four years, my skill set expanded to include some pretty unexpected items such as: farm management, farm animal care and living off the grid (which is a skill on its own).


I played with a few local musical artists in the city which put me in a few festivals and even got me on TV a few times.


Farm life proved to be very peaceful but not so satisfying. I missed New York. I was determined to move back home and find a creative line of work where I could make a decent salary. I always loved computers and using my creativity to interact with them, so I thought coding would be a great option.


At first, the idea of taking on such a huge body of knowledge was very intimidating, especially since it seemed so unrelated to anything I had ever done before. I began taking some online courses in html and css and got a rush of excitement doing something as simple as changing text color. I discovered how much I enjoyed solving problems and how I had actually been a problem solver my whole life. I began to see how creative coding was, how it related to my other skills and how algorithms are a part of everyday life. This fascinated me. To me, coding is about infinite possibility, creativity and connecting with your inner superhero!

UX Remote Control Redesign

THE PROBLEM:
This remote offers many functions and tries to show all the functions on one remote, which is overwhelming. Of the few quality DVD players still on the market, this is one of the few that allows the user so much control over their recording with editing, documenting for archiving and multiple viewing modes.

BASIC USER: They are not interested in the more complex functionality offered with this remote and prefer not to even see it- they can’t imagine who would use those functions.
Habits: They get their other media mostly from their digital devices but still have some old movies/family vids on DVD that they like to watch occasionally.
Needs/Desires/Expectations: This user wants to use the basic functions of the device. They just want to watch a DVD. Habits: They get most of their media interaction from the internet and don’t want to be bothered with complex remotes or visually overwhelming remotes.

PRO USER: Usually in an industry that still deals with DVD editing and or recording of DVD’s.
Needs/Desires/Expectations: Wants a way to easily edit, title, and manipulate recorded material in the simplest quickest way possible.
Habits: Records for multiple hours weekly and fills orders (customized DVD’s) for clients.
Main complaints about current model:
1. There is no QWERTY keyboard so inputting and editing window titles is very time consuming and frustrating.
2. Using the multiple rewind and FF speeds can confuse the remote and many times user ends up passing their desired location.

PROPOSED SOLUTION: A multi-view cell app that is used as a remote. One for basic user view and one for Pro user view.

Features of Basic User:
Power
Rewind/FF
Play/Stop

Features of the Pro User:
Power
Rewind /FF different speeds (jog shuttle)
Play/Stop
Input Select
Return
Direct Nav
Enter
Function (Finalize DVD)
Record
